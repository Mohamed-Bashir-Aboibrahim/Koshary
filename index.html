What is Koshary?

Koshary (also spelled Koshari, Kushari) is Egypt’s national dish and ultimate street food. It’s a hearty, satisfying bowl of rice, lentils, pasta, chickpeas, spicy tomato sauce, and crispy fried onions, all mixed together to create a symphony of flavors and textures.
From humble beginnings as a working-class meal, Koshary has become a symbol of Egyptian hospitality, unity, and culinary creativity!

Did you know? Koshary is 100% vegan and packed with protein!

The Story of Koshary

1800s: Colonial Inspiration

Koshary’s roots may trace to British-Indian “khichri” (rice & lentils), brought to Egypt by colonial soldiers. Egyptians made it their own by adding pasta, tomato sauce, and fried onions.

1920s: Egyptian Street Food

Koshary appears in Cairo food stalls, quickly becoming a staple for workers and students—affordable, filling, and vegetarian-friendly.

Modern Day: National Dish

Today, Koshary is enjoyed by all Egyptians—rich and poor, young and old—and is served everywhere from street carts to fancy restaurants!
